2. Source Exclusion Requirements
The objective of the collection is to obtain publicly available analytical and educational content from a broad
range of organizations while avoiding highly represented institutional sources.
The following sources should be excluded unless explicit written approval is provided:
1
Excluded Source Type
Requirement
Fortune 500
Companies
Do not collect or deliver files originating from Fortune 500 companies or their
subsidiaries.
Elite U.S. Universities
Avoid content originating from major U.S. universities and institutions with
significant public visibility and extensive online publication archives.
Prestigious U.S.
Research Centers
Avoid content originating from leading U.S. research laboratories, federally
funded research centers, think tanks, or nationally recognized research
organizations.
Explicitly Blocklisted
Organizations
Any organization added to future exclusion lists must be excluded from
collection and delivery.
Examples include large corporate investor-relations presentations, executive strategy decks, flagship
university course materials, and research publications from highly represented U.S. institutions.
The delivery team should prioritize content from diverse organizations, industry groups, regional
institutions, professional associations, independent publishers, government agencies, and non-flagship
educational institutions.
3. Metadata Preservation Requirements
Metadata preservation is a critical requirement of the delivery process.
Mandatory Requirement
The source URL must always be preserved.
If the source URL is missing, the file should be considered incomplete and unsuitable for delivery.
Preferred Metadata Fields
The delivery team should preserve all available metadata whenever possible, including:

Delivery Principle
Retain all metadata by default.
The downstream team can remove unnecessary fields later; however, metadata that is not preserved during
collection cannot be recovered afterward.
The collection pipeline should therefore be configured to maximize metadata retention while minimizing
manual intervention.

5. Document Quality and Interface Requirements
All delivered files must maintain a professional graphical presentation quality.
The objective is to collect documents that communicate information visually and analytically rather than
through large volumes of plain text.
